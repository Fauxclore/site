Kurpu di Mundu

In 2025, I have had the pleasure and privilege of collaborating with the Ordem do Ó in developing sound design for their ballets. The one referred to in this article was called Kurpu di Mundu, and was developed in partnership between Pedro Ramos (from Portugal) and Ernesto Nambera (from Guinea-Bissau). It was a challenge unlike any other I had accepted before. Composing music for a contemporary ballet took me into musical fields that were different from my usual language. Specifically, I was able to develop pieces like the one you find at the beginning of this article, but also other more textural pieces like Cigarras:

And even more rhythmic ones, specific to intense choreographic moments. In this case, I called the more rhythmic music ‘Reich’, in honour of one of the composers who most influenced me, Steve Reich.
